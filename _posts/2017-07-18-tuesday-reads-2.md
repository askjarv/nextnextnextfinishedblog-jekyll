For years the specialist press has talked about security issues but now we're really seeing the impact thanks to [Petya.](http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/technology-40645569) The lessons learned from this are hopefully ones everyone already knows (patching) but I still see people who are apathetic around this topic - it's not rocket science people, accept that patches are a fact of life and plan for them, not hope you don't get caught out.

Rock Paper Shotgun totally stole one of my ideas (I'll let them off with it) with [Curated Stores](https://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2017/07/17/rps-suggests-game-stores-and-sites-that-say-no/?utm_source=feedburner&utm_medium=feed&utm_campaign=Feed%3A+RockPaperShotgun+%28Rock%2C+Paper%2C+Shotgun%29): I think there's a great opportunity for some custom front ends with specialisms in Steam but I also think there are challenges with any such approach (discover-ability and making them sustainable (e.g. pay the bills)).
